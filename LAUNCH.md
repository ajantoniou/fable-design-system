## First comment (the maker's comment — this is what people actually read)

> 👋 I'm Dr. Antoniou — a physician who builds. I kept noticing that every AI
> coding agent, no matter how good at code, designs the same way: default fonts,
> flat white backgrounds, generic Tailwind, that unmistakable "an AI made this"
> look.
>
> The **fable** coding model was different — its UI had a real designer's eye:
> warm editorial palettes, deliberate typography, motion that earned its keep,
> and it actually *critiqued its own designs by measuring contrast and spacing*
> instead of eyeballing.
>
> So I reconstructed that instinct into a **free, drop-in skill** for **Claude,
> Codex, and Antigravity**. Install it once, and your agent stops shipping the
> default template and starts designing like it has taste — declaring a system,
> picking real type pairings, using one meaningful accent, verifying in a real
> browser.
>
> It's **100% free**, MIT-licensed, no signup. Built for **creatives and content
> creators** and offered free by **[UploadCheck](https://www.uploadcheck.app)**
> (my pre-publish QC tool for video & podcasts).
>
> 👉 Install in 30 seconds: https://github.com/ajantoniou/fable-design-system
>
> Would genuinely love feedback on the design tokens — what reads as "Fable" to
> you and what doesn't. ⭐ the repo if it saves you time. 🎨
